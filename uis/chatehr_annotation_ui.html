Help Us Evaluate and Improve ChatEHR For Summarization Queries

We're developing a new way to automatically assess summaries generated by the ChatEHR UI. Your clinical expertise is vital to ensure this evaluation is accurate and clinically relevant.

Step by Step Guide:

  1. In Epic, open a patient's chart. This should be a patient you are familiar with and are caring for or have cared for in the past.
  2. Launch the ChatEHR UI.
  3. Under "Select data sources", click Select All.
  4. Consider asking a different prompt to ChatEHR for each patient. You can use the prompts below, or your own prompts.

  5. Review the output of the ChatEHR query. Identify any:
    • Inaccuracies: information that is incorrect, contradicts with the chart, or misrepresents certainty (e.g., "probable diagnosis" became "definite").
    • Hallucinations: information that is not supported by the chart or is not supported by the patient's medical history.
    • Omissions: information that should be in the summary and is supported by the chart or the patient's medical history. Please do not spend more than 5 minutes on this task per patient. The goal is not to find all possible omissions, but to identify ones that could result in downstream consequences in patient care.
  6. For each patient, please fill out the form below:
    • How familiar are you with the patient?
    • What is the primary clinical task you are trying to accomplish with this query?
    • Then copy/paste identified issues (inaccuracies, hallucinations, omissions), one row for each issue, and their corresponding risk score (refer to FAQ for risk score definitions).
  7. If you are unable to identify any issues, please click "No issues found, copy to clipboard". Otherwise, click "Save feedback, copy to clipboard".
  8. Go back to the ChatEHR UI, click 👎 and paste in the feedback field. Submit in the ChatEHR UI.
  9. Click "Clear input for next patient". And repeat the process for the next patient!
▶ FAQ:
  • Level 1 (No harm): The output contains no clinically meaningful errors that would affect patient care.
  • Level 2 (Mild harm): Bodily or psychological injury resulting in minimal symptoms or loss of function, or injury limited to additional treatment, monitoring, and/or increased length of stay.
  • Level 3 (Moderate harm): Bodily or psychological injury, adversely affecting functional ability or quality of life, but not at the level of severe harm.
  • Level 4 (Severe harm): Bodily or psychological injury, including pain or disfigurement, that interferes substantially with functional ability or quality of life.
  • Level 5 (Death): The errors could lead to patient death.
